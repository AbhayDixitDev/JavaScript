Arithmetic assignments

Let's create a variable d and assign it the value 10.

Then, let's use the arithmetic assignment operators to perform calculations on d.

  • d += 5; is the same as d = d + 5;
  • d -= 5; is the same as d = d - 5;
  • d \*= 5; is the same as d = d * 5;
  • d /= 5; is the same as d = d / 5;
  • d %= 5; is the same as d = d % 5;
  • d **= 5; is the same as d = d ** 5;

Comparison operators

Let's create a variable e and assign it the value 10.

Then, let's use the comparison operators to compare e with 10.

  • e == 10 checks if e is equal to 10.
  • e === 10 checks if e is equal to 10 and if they are the same type.
  • e != 10 checks if e is not equal to 10.
  • e !== 10 checks if e is not equal to 10 and if they are not the same type.
  • e < 10 checks if e is less than 10.
  • e > 10 checks if e is greater than 10.
  • e <= 10 checks if e is less than or equal to 10.
  • e >= 10 checks if e is greater than or equal to 10.

Logical operators

Let's create two variables f and g and assign them the values 10 and 20, respectively.

Then, let's use the logical operators to perform logical operations on f and g.

  • f > 10 && g > 10 checks if both f and g are greater than 10.
  • f > 10 || g > 10 checks if either f or g is greater than 10.
  • !(f > 10) checks if f is not greater than 10.
